About Rootcare

Founded in 2010 by master horticulturist Elena Greenfield, Rootcare has grown from a small boutique garden design studio to a nationally recognized leader in organic landscape architecture. What began as a passion project with just two employees has blossomed into a team of 28 dedicated professionals including landscape architects, soil scientists, permaculture specialists, and ecological restoration experts.

Our philosophy centers on the belief that gardens should exist in harmony with their natural surroundings. We specialize in creating spaces that promote biodiversity while providing beauty and functionality. Each of our projects begins with a comprehensive site analysis where we assess soil composition, microclimates, existing vegetation, and water flow patterns. This scientific approach ensures our designs work with nature rather than against it.

Over the past decade, we've designed and installed over 600 gardens across the Northeast, ranging from small urban oases to expansive rural estates. Our work has been featured in numerous publications including Organic Gardening Magazine, Landscape Architecture Digest, and The New York Times Home & Garden section.

Our Mission

To create sustainable outdoor spaces that nurture both people and the planet through regenerative design practices that improve ecosystem health while meeting human needs.

Gardening Tips

Choose Native Plants

Opt for native plants that require less water and maintenance, promoting local biodiversity.

Implement Composting

Enrich your soil naturally by composting, reducing waste and improving plant health.

Use Mulch

Retain moisture and suppress weeds by using mulch, creating a healthier garden environment.

Plan Your Layout

Maximize sunlight exposure by planning your garden layout, ensuring all plants receive adequate light.

Check for Pests

Regularly check for pests and diseases to maintain plant health, using organic methods whenever possible.

Rotate Crops

Prevent soil depletion and reduce pest buildup by rotating crops each season.

Incorporate Companion Planting

Enhance growth and deter pests naturally by utilizing companion planting techniques.

Utilize Rain Barrels

Conserve resources and reduce costs by collecting rainwater for irrigation.
